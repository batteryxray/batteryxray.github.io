Welcome to our research group!

Our group focuses on developing advanced synchrotron X-ray imaging and spectroscopy techniques to reveal how energy materials evolve structurally and chemically under realistic operating conditions. By combining cutting-edge synchrotron instrumentation with quantitative imaging and spectroscopy approaches, we aim to uncover the fundamental mechanisms that govern the performance, degradation, and transformation of batteries and other electrochemical energy systems.

Our research integrates a range of advanced characterization methods, including transmission X-ray microscopy (TXM), nano-computed tomography (nano-CT), and two- and three-dimensional X-ray absorption near-edge structure (2D/3D XANES) mapping. These techniques enable us to visualize and quantify structural, chemical, and electrochemical transformations across multiple length scales, providing new insights into the complex processes that control energy storage and conversion materials.

Building on extensive expertise in synchrotron instrumentation and beamline development at the Advanced Photon Source (APS), Argonne National Laboratory (ANL), our group is committed to advancing next-generation characterization methodologies while addressing critical challenges in sustainable energy technologies.

Location: Zijingang campus, Zhejiang University, Hangzhou, China

Join Us: We are always interested in working with motivated students, postdoctoral researchers, and collaborators who share our passion for synchrotron science, advanced characterization, and sustainable energy techniques.

Active Projects

Cathode Materials

We explore the structural and chemical evolution of high-energy-density layered oxide cathodes, including Ni-rich and Li- and Mn-rich materials, to reveal the fundamental mechanisms controlling energy density, degradation, and lifetime.

View Publication

Solid-State Batteries

We investigate interfacial phenomena, device architectures, and degradation pathways in solid-state batteries through advanced characterization approaches to accelerate the development of next-generation energy storage technologies.
